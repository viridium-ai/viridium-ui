The value chain

Summary

Overall, the value chain is designed to ensure that all stakeholders, including the company, its customers, and its business partners, receive the highest level of value and service possible. This requires careful coordination of all activities, from infrastructure design and construction to day-to-day operations and customer service.

For example, the value chain for an airport refers to the set of activities that take place to create and deliver value to passengers, airlines, and other stakeholders.

There are many ways of grouping these activities. A common taxonomy would be based on business units in a company. The categories can vary, for Viridium AI, they are grouped for the purpose for sustainability.

For example, for an airport, the following categories can be used.

Categories for an Airport

Infrastructure

This includes the design, construction, and maintenance of the airport's physical infrastructure, such as runways, taxiways, terminals, parking facilities, and other buildings and structures.

Operations

This involves the day-to-day activities of running an airport, such as air traffic control, baggage handling, security, cleaning, and maintenance.

Customer service

This includes all activities related to passenger experience, such as check-in, security screening, boarding, retail, food and beverage, and other amenities.

Commercial activities

This includes all activities related to generating revenue from airlines, tenants, and other customers, such as leasing space to airlines and retail tenants, parking fees, and other ancillary services.

Support services

This includes all activities that are necessary to support the core functions of the airport, such as finance, HR, legal, IT, and marketing.

Sub categories

While the value measurements at top level categories are required, it is not typically obtained at this level, rather they are aggregated up from sub categories where the specific information needed for measurements are available. This categorization can and often is hierarchical, that forms a taxonomy tree for the value chain activities.

Partner's value chain

To get full picture of the sustainability impact of a company, its partner's value chain also plays a role. This is because the cost of the product and or service by the partners have direct impact on the cost of the product or service provided by the company. For example, Airline that uses the airport, construction companies that build runway. coffee shops that rent the retain space etc. It is often very hard to collect such information, but with proper disclosure rules, and a common platform it could become a possibility.

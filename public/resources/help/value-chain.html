The Value Chain

Summary

The value chain is designed to ensure that all stakeholders (company, customers, and business partners) receive the highest level of value and service possible. To achieve this, activities ranging from infrastructure design and construction to daily operations and customer service must all be carefully coordinated.

While there are many ways of grouping these activities, Viridium AI groups these categories based on sustainability goals.

To continue with the airport example, the following categories can be used:

Categories for an airport

Infrastructure

This includes the design, construction, and maintenance of the airport's physical infrastructure, such as: runways, taxiways, terminals, parking facilities, and other buildings and structures.

Operations

This involves the day-to-day activities of running an airport, such as: air traffic control, baggage handling, security, cleaning, and maintenance.

Customer service

This includes all activities related to the passenger experience, such as: check-in, security screening, boarding, retail, food and beverage, and other amenities.

Commercial activities

This includes all activities related to generating revenue from airlines, tenants, and other customers, such as: leasing space to airlines and retail tenants, parking fees, and other ancillary services.

Support services

This includes all activities that are necessary to support the core functions of the airport, such as: finance, HR, legal, IT, and marketing.

Subcategories

Value measurements at top level categories are quite complex as they require a lot of data from many different sources. Accurate measurements are obtained through aggregating subcategories, as they have the data for each specific category. As a result, value chains are often hierarchical, much like a taxonomy tree used for classifying living things.

Partners' Value Chain

In order to get the full picture of a company's sustainability impact, you also have to look at the role its partners' value chain plays. For example, an airline will also have to take into consideration the construction companies that built the runway, along with the coffee shops that rent the airport's retail space. While it is often very difficult to collect this data, with proper disclosure rules and a common platform, it could become a possibility.
